Skin basics

A calmer way to understand your face.

Most daily skincare decisions come back to a few basics: gentle cleansing, moisturising, sun protection, and knowing when a skin concern needs a clinician.

Keep the barrier boring

A gentle cleanser and moisturiser can reduce friction, dryness, and irritation. Mayo Clinic skin-care guidance outlines this baseline.

Protect against UV

FDA and CDC guidance both point to broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 15 or higher to reduce sunburn and aging.

Safety first

Useful guidance has limits.

●

Confidence can organise visible skin signals and routine ideas, but it does not diagnose conditions.

●

Seek medical advice for sudden, painful, infected, bleeding, changing, or persistent concerns.

●

Patch test new products and introduce active ingredients slowly, especially if your skin is reactive.

●

Sun protection, gentle cleansing, and moisturising remain the baseline for most routines.
