Skin basics

A calmer way to understand your face.

Most daily skincare decisions come back to a few basics: gentle cleansing, moisturising, sun protection, and knowing when a skin concern needs a clinician rather than another product.

Keep the barrier boring

A gentle cleanser and moisturiser can reduce friction, dryness, and irritation. Mayo Clinic skin-care guidance emphasises gentle cleansing and moisturising as foundations for healthy skin.

Mayo Clinic

Protect against UV

FDA and CDC guidance both point to broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 15 or higher as part of reducing sunburn, skin-cancer risk, and early skin aging from UV exposure.

FDA sun safety

Track patterns, not panic

Photo-based tools can help you organise observations over time, such as dryness, oiliness, and visible changes. They should support your judgment, not replace medical assessment.

AAD skin-care basics

Escalate the right things

If a concern is severe, painful, spreading, bleeding, infected, or not improving, Confidence nudges you toward professional care rather than treating the app result as a diagnosis.

CDC prevention
Safety first

Useful guidance has limits.

Confidence can organise visible skin signals and routine ideas, but it does not diagnose conditions.

Seek medical advice for sudden, painful, infected, bleeding, changing, or persistent concerns.

Patch test new products and introduce active ingredients slowly, especially if your skin is reactive.
